Why Are You Entering This Space?

Project 4: Protocol

Site

The site is the entrance area of the Industrial Design space in N25. It is a place that appears open, but is mostly used by people who already have a reason to enter.

Protocol

The protocol of this site is not written on a sign. People who belong to the space enter naturally and without hesitation. They usually have a clear purpose: class, studio work, or a meeting.

Others may enter because they are curious, or because they are following someone else. In that case, the act of entering becomes uncertain. The difference between belonging and not belonging appears in the moment before entering.

What I Wanted to Reveal

I wanted to make this invisible protocol visible. Instead of asking who is allowed to enter, I wanted to ask why someone is entering.

Why are you entering this space?

Gesture

I added a digital intervention. When visitors open the webpage, they are given three seconds to choose their reason for entering:

Class / Studio Work / Meeting / Just curious / Following someone

If they choose Class, Studio Work, or Meeting, the page recognizes them as someone who already belongs to the protocol of the space. If they choose Just curious, Following someone, or fail to answer within three seconds, the page reveals their uncertainty.

Why This Addition?

I did not want to block the entrance physically. A barrier would change the function of the site too directly. Instead, I used a short question and a timer to create a small moment of self-awareness.

The gesture is minimal. It does not stop people from entering. It only asks them to recognize their reason for entering.

Impact

The intervention created a short pause before entry. Some people could immediately choose a clear reason. Others hesitated, and that hesitation became the main evidence of the protocol.

Through the webpage, the site became less automatic. Entering became a decision rather than a habit.
